用于实验室啮齿动物传染病血清学监测的多重免疫层析检测试纸条的长期储存稳定性。

实验室啮齿动物传染病的血清学监测对于确保动物健康至关重要。因此，我们之前开发了一种多重免疫层析法（ICA）来检测针对以下六种病原体的抗体：小鼠肝炎病毒、仙台病毒和毛状梭菌（泰泽氏病），以及大鼠汉城型正汉坦病毒、仙台病毒和唾液腺炎病毒。然而，这些条带的制备既费时又费力。因此，本研究旨在确定所开发的多重 ICA 条的长期储存条件。无论是否存在干燥剂、氧气吸收剂或真空状态，在 -30°C 下储存在聚乙烯拉链密封袋 (poly_bags) 中的多重 ICA 条对所有六种目标病原体均保持可检测的反应性 36 个月。然而，当储存在 18-28 °C（室温）和 4 °C 时，储存在 Poly_bags 中的多重 ICA 试纸条对于六种目标病原体中的一些的可检测反应性在储存后 ​​18 个月时有所降低。使用干燥剂和真空密封塑料袋（真空袋）储存并不能阻止这种减少。氧吸收剂降低了多重 ICA 条的可检测性。这些结果表明，将 ICA 试纸条储存在 -30 °C 的塑料袋中是一种简单的方法，能够长期保持本研究中检查的试纸条格式和病原体目标的可检测性。

Long-term storage stability of multiplex immunochromatographic assay strips for serologic monitoring of infectious diseases in laboratory rodents.

Serological monitoring of infectious diseases in laboratory rodents is vital for ensuring animal health. Therefore, we have previously developed a multiplex immunochromatographic assay (ICA) to detect antibodies against six pathogens as follows: mouse hepatitis virus, Sendai virus, and Clostridium piliforme (Tyzzer's disease) in mice, and Seoul type orthohantavirus, Sendai virus, and sialodacryoadenitis virus in rats. However, the preparation of these strips is time-consuming and labor-intensive. Therefore, this study aimed to determine the long-term storage conditions for the developed multiplex ICA strips. The multiplex ICA strips stored in the polyethylene zipper-sealed bags (poly_bags) at -30 °C maintained detectable reactivity for all six targeted pathogens for 36 months, regardless of the presence or absence of desiccants, oxygen absorbers, or a vacuum state. However, when stored at 18-28 °C (room temperature) and 4 °C, the detectable reactivity of the multiplex ICA strips stored in poly_bags was reduced for some of the six targets pathogens at 18 months after storage. This reduction was not prevented by the storage with the desiccants and in the vacuum-sealed poly_bags (vacuum_bags). The oxygen absorber reduced detectability of the multiplex ICA strips. These results indicate that storing ICA strips in poly_bags at -30 °C, which is a simple method, is able to maintain detectability long-term for the strips format and pathogen targets examined in this study.

智利首次对人源安第斯山脉病毒进行全国性全基因组表征：回顾性基因组流行病学研究。

安第斯病毒（ANDV）是已知的唯一可在人与人之间传播的汉坦病毒，并在智利和阿根廷引起汉坦病毒心肺综合征。在智利，ANDV 基因组多样性仍未完全表征。本研究旨在利用 13 年期间（2011-24）收集的人类临床样本来表征 ANDV 的遗传多样性、地理结构和分子特征。我们对智利 ANDV 感染进行了回顾性基因组流行病学研究。对 2011 年 3 月 9 日至 2024 年 6 月 27 日期间收集的确诊 ANDV 患者的临床样本进行了分析和测序。临床和流行病学数据来自诊断实验室和监测项目。生成了 S、M 和 L 片段的共有序列，并使用系统发育推断和变体调用来评估遗传聚类和分歧。我们分析了 58 名感染者的临床样本，并确定了 ANDV 的两种主要基因组变异，它们具有不同的地理分布，由 S、M 和 L 区段的核苷酸和氨基酸取代的区域结构模式定义：ANDV Chi-North（智利中部）和 ANDV-South（智利南部）。没有观察到按临床严重程度进行一致的聚类，并且没有反复出现的非同义替换与严重疾病唯一相关。此前在阿根廷疫情中与人际传播相关的替代现象在智利序列中并未得到一致观察，包括四例人际传播病例。尽管 ANDV 样病毒中描述的一些替换存在于 Chi-North 谱系中，但该谱系在系统发育上仍然不同，并且在地理上仅限于智利中部。据我们所知，这项研究首次提供了智利全国范围内人源性 ANDV 的基因组特征。地理结构变异的识别表明，智利的 ANDV 多样性是由区域多样化而非临床结果驱动的。缺乏与疾病严重程度或人际传播相关的一致氨基酸特征表明，这些表型不太可能仅用病毒遗传变异来解释。这些发现完善了目前对 ANDV 进化的理解，并强调了在流行地区持续进行综合基因组监测的必要性。智利国家调查局和国立卫生研究院。

First nationwide full-genome characterisation of human-derived Andes virus in Chile: a retrospective genomic epidemiology study.

Andes virus (ANDV) is the only hantavirus known to transmit between humans and causes hantavirus cardiopulmonary syndrome in Chile and Argentina. In Chile, ANDV genomic diversity remains incompletely characterised. This study aimed to characterise the genetic diversity, geographical structure, and molecular signatures of ANDV using human clinical samples collected over a 13-year period (2011-24). We conducted a retrospective genomic epidemiology study of ANDV infections in Chile. Clinical samples from patients with confirmed ANDV, collected between March 9, 2011, and June 27, 2024, were analysed and sequenced. Clinical and epidemiological data were obtained from diagnostic laboratories and surveillance programmes. Consensus sequences for the S, M, and L segments were generated, and genetic clustering and divergence were assessed using phylogenetic inference and variant calling. We analysed clinical samples from 58 infected individuals and identified two major genomic variants of ANDV with distinct geographical distributions, defined by regionally structured patterns of nucleotide and amino acid substitutions across the S, M, and L segments: ANDV Chi-North (central Chile) and ANDV-South (southern Chile). No consistent clustering by clinical severity was observed, and no recurrent non-synonymous substitutions were uniquely associated with severe disease. Substitutions previously associated with person-to-person transmission in outbreaks in Argentina were not consistently observed in Chilean sequences, including in four person-to-person transmission cases. Although some substitutions described in ANDV-like viruses were present in the Chi-North lineage, this lineage remained phylogenetically distinct and geographically restricted to central Chile. To our knowledge, this study provides the first nationwide genomic characterisation of human-derived ANDV in Chile. The identification of geographically structured variants indicates that ANDV diversity in Chile is driven by regional diversification rather than clinical outcome. The absence of consistent amino acid signatures associated with disease severity or person-to-person transmission suggests that these phenotypes are unlikely to be explained by viral genetic variation alone. These findings refine current understanding of ANDV evolution and highlight the need for continued integrated genomic surveillance in endemic regions. Agencia Nacional de Investigación y Desarrollo de Chile and National Institutes of Health.

Andes Virus - A Clinical Review.

Andes virus (ANDV) is the sole orthohantavirus with documented human-to-human transmission. We summarize the epidemiology and clinical features of ANDV infection and review best practices in clinical management, as based on published expert consensus guidelines, field experience, and clinical trials. We also evaluate currently available and investigational treatments (including the use of antiviral agents), assess emerging monoclonal antibody therapies, and outline prospects for vaccine development. Finally, we discuss important infection prevention and control measures.

肾综合征出血热出血：对汉坦病毒感染止血丧失特征的系统评价。

肾综合征出血热 (HFRS) 由汉坦病毒引起，与不同程度的血小板减少、凝血异常和出血表现相关。虽然多项研究已经描述了临床特征，但之前没有系统评价综合 HFRS 止血功能障碍的证据。本综述旨在描述 HFRS 凝血障碍的临床和实验室特征，为了解疾病机制和未来研究提供信息。我们于 2024 年 12 月 12 日系统地检索了 MEDLINE、PubMed、CINAHL、Web of Science 和 Scopus，以查找报告确诊 HFRS 中止血功能障碍的临床或实验室证据的研究。该审查方案已在 PROSPERO 上前瞻性注册（CRD42024618760）。符合预定纳入标准的观察性研究和病例系列是合格的。使用乔安娜·布里格斯研究所 (JBI) 关键评估工具和美国国立卫生研究院 (NIH) 质量评估工具（对于一项前后队列研究）评估偏倚风险。根据方法学质量，研究从 1（最低）到 3（最高）分级。由于研究设计和结果报告的异质性，荟萃分析不可行。计算实验室参数的加权平均值，并使用气泡图和条形图可视化数据。纳入了 55 项研究，涉及 7,950 名患者。血小板减少症是最一致的异常，通常在汉坦 (HTNV)、多布拉瓦 (DOBV) 和首尔 (SEOV) 病毒感染中比普马拉 (PUUV) 病毒感染更为明显，后者与较轻的疾病相关。据报道，活化部分凝血活酶时间轻度延长，D-二聚体升高。有限的儿科数据表明疾病较轻，凝血异常不太明显。本综述首次对 HFRS 的止血功能障碍进行了系统综合分析。研究结果强调，与 PUUV 相比，HTNV、DOBV 和 SEOV 感染的血小板减少和出血更为严重。研究质量参差不齐和报告不完整限制了确切的结论，强调了对凝血和出血结果进行标准化、前瞻性研究的必要性。

Bleeding in haemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome: A systematic review characterising the loss of haemostasis in hantavirus infections.

Haemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome (HFRS) is caused by hantaviruses and is associated with variable thrombocytopenia, coagulation abnormalities, and bleeding manifestations. While clinical features have been described in multiple studies, no previous systematic review has synthesised evidence on haemostatic dysfunction in HFRS. This review aimed to characterise the clinical and laboratory features of coagulation disturbances in HFRS to inform understanding of disease mechanisms and future research. We systematically searched MEDLINE, PubMed, CINAHL, Web of Science, and Scopus on 12 December 2024 for studies reporting clinical or laboratory evidence of haemostatic dysfunction in confirmed HFRS. The review protocol was prospectively registered on PROSPERO (CRD42024618760). Observational studies and case series meeting predefined inclusion criteria were eligible. Risk of bias was assessed using the Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) critical appraisal tools and, for one before-after cohort study, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) quality assessment tool. Studies were graded from 1 (lowest) to 3 (highest) based on methodological quality. Due to heterogeneity in study design and outcome reporting, meta-analysis was not feasible. Weighted averages were calculated for laboratory parameters, and data were visualised using bubble and bar plots. Fifty-five studies comprising 7,950 patients were included. Thrombocytopenia was the most consistent abnormality, typically more pronounced in infections with Hantaan (HTNV), Dobrava (DOBV), and Seoul (SEOV) viruses compared with Puumala (PUUV), which was associated with milder disease. Mild prolongation of activated partial thromboplastin time and elevated D-dimer were variably reported. Limited paediatric data suggested milder disease and less pronounced coagulation abnormalities. This review provides the first systematic synthesis of haemostatic dysfunction in HFRS. Findings highlight more severe thrombocytopenia and bleeding in HTNV, DOBV, and SEOV infections compared with PUUV. Variable study quality and incomplete reporting limit firm conclusions, underscoring the need for standardised, prospective studies of coagulation and bleeding outcomes.

汉坦病毒感染的神经表现：综述。

汉坦病毒最近引起了几次人类疾病的爆发。它们的死亡率很高，有些物种可以在人与人之间传播，尽管它们通常是从啮齿动物传播给人类。神经系统并发症已有记录，但人们对其知之甚少。概括地说，有两种类型的综合征与汉坦病毒有关。肺部综合症死亡率很高，并可能导致肺和心力衰竭。神经系统受累通常继发于缺氧和缺血性损伤或代谢功能障碍。与某些病毒种类相关的感染的另一种表现称为肾综合征出血热。一些患者可能会出现脑膜脑炎或垂体卒中，导致突然视力丧失和垂体功能障碍。极少数情况下，可能会发生癫痫、脊髓炎和周围神经病变。尽管没有批准用于治疗汉坦病毒的特定抗病毒药物，但有几种在临床前试验中显示出疗效。管理是支持性护理和症状治疗。汉坦病毒感染的神经系统表现并不常见，但可能很严重。需要前瞻性研究和实验模型来更好地表征这些表现、了解病理生理学、确定治疗靶点并制定治疗指南。

Neurological Manifestations of Hantavirus Infection: A Review.

Hantaviruses have caused several recent human disease outbreaks. They carry a high mortality rate, and some species can spread from person to person although they are typically spread from rodents to humans. Neurological complications have been documented but are poorly understood. Broadly, 2 types of syndromes have been associated with hantaviruses. The pulmonary syndrome carries a high mortality rate and can result in pulmonary and cardiac failure. Neurological involvement is typically secondary to hypoxic and ischemic injury or metabolic dysfunction. Another manifestation of the infection that is associated with some species of the virus is termed hemorrhagic fever with renal syndrome. Some patients may develop a meningoencephalitis or pituitary apoplexy resulting in sudden visual loss and pituitary dysfunction. Rarely, seizures, myelitis, and peripheral neuropathy may occur. Although there are no specific antiviral drugs approved for treatment of hantavirus, there are several that showed efficacy in preclinical trials. Management is supportive care and treatment of symptoms. Neurological manifestations of hantavirus infection are uncommon but can be severe. Prospective studies and experimental models are needed to better characterize these manifestations, understand pathophysiology, identify therapeutic targets, and develop guidelines for management.

欧洲森林和城市绿地宿主病原体群落组合的驱动因素。

尽管对传染病的了解取得了进展，但野生动物病原体的持续存在和重新出现继续引起公众和兽医的健康担忧。本研究调查了欧洲生物多样性与啮齿动物传播疾病之间的关系，重点关注栖息地改变及其对啮齿动物多样性的影响。我们提供了来自五个欧洲国家 21 个温带森林地点和 8 个城市绿地的宿主病原体数据，这些环境中啮齿动物丰富，人类/家养动物与野生动物可能发生相互作用。从 2020 年到 2022 年，对包含 15 种不同小型哺乳动物的 3766 个样本进行了分析。通过 16S rRNA 测序或 PCR 筛选样本中的细菌，并使用免疫荧光检测筛选病毒抗体。在 11 个宿主物种中检测到来自多个属的病原体，包括巴尔通体、疏螺旋体、支原体、无形体、新埃利希体、钩端螺旋体、正汉坦病毒和正痘病毒。栖息地之间的宿主群落组成有所不同，公园中的城市适应者比森林中的更多。病原体丰富度随着宿主物种多样性的增加而增加，支持“宿主多样性产生寄生虫多样性”的假说，尽管与人类化无关。城市地区缺乏一些媒介传播的寄生虫，表明人类影响导致病原体群落发生变化。宿主物种和内在因素是支原体物种和肉囊虫科的主要解释变量，而外在环境和气候因素影响了几种媒介传播病原体的变化。黑线姬鼠和光光密纹鼠分别是城市空间和温带森林中的重要连接宿主。这些结果提高了我们对复杂的当地宿主病原体系统的理解，有助于未来的管理决策并支持公共卫生部门。

Drivers of Host-Pathogen Community Assemblages in European Forests and Urban Green Spaces.

Despite advances in understanding infectious diseases, the persistence and re-emergence of wildlife pathogens continue to raise public and veterinary health concerns. This study investigates the relationship between biodiversity and rodent-borne diseases in Europe, focusing on habitat alterations and their impact on rodent diversity. We present host-pathogen data from 21 temperate forest sites and eight urban green spaces throughout five European countries, environments where rodents are abundant and human/domestic animals-wildlife interactions are likely to occur. From 2020 to 2022, 3766 specimens comprising 15 different small mammal species were analyzed. Samples were screened for bacteria via 16S rRNA sequencing or PCR, and for viral antibodies using immunofluorescent assays. Pathogens from several genera, including Bartonella, Borrelia, Mycoplasma, Anaplasma, Neoehrlichia, Leptospira, Orthohantavirus, and Orthopoxvirus, were detected at non-negligible prevalence in 11 host species. Host community composition differed between habitats, with more urban adapters in parks than in forests. Pathogen richness increased with an increase in host species diversity, supporting the "host-diversity begets parasite-diversity" hypothesis, though not with anthropization. The absence of some vector-transmitted parasites in urban areas suggests a shift in pathogen community driven by human impact. Host species and intrinsic factors were dominant explanatory variables for Mycoplasma species and Sarcocystidae, while extrinsic environmental and climatic factors influenced variations in several vector-transmitted pathogens. Apodemus sylvaticus and Clethrionomys glareolus served as important connector hosts in urban spaces and temperate forests, respectively. These results improve our understanding of the complex local host-pathogen system, aiding future management decisions and supporting the public health sector.
